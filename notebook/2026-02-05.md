# 2026-02-05

## Objective
Finalize the Project Proposal and submit the Request for Approval (RFA).

## Record of what was done
- Collaborated with Sandra and Jonathan to outline the core problem: current automated cleaners waste water and power by cleaning blindly on a schedule.
- Drafted the High-Level Requirements, specifically the requirement that the MCU must detect a >25% power dip and attribute it to dirt vs. shade before initiating a cleaning cycle.
- Finalized the block diagram, showing the ESP32-WROOM-32U managing the MPPT logic, locomotion (via motor drivers), and the cleaning module.
- Submitted the formal proposal to TA Chihun Song.

## Key decisions
- Use voltage and current sensing for per-panel power tracking rather than relying solely on a single string measurement.
- Cross-reference power data with ambient light to disambiguate between dirty panels and cloudy weather.
- Original MCU choice was the ESP32-WROOM-32U; this will be revisited later.
