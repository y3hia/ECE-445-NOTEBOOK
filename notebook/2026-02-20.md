## Record of what was done
Held a group PCB review session to verify our schematic before the upcoming order deadline.

### MCU Pivot
Realized the ESP32-WROOM-32 does not have enough usable I/O pins for all our sensors, motor PWMs, and MPPT control. Switched our main MCU to the ESP32-S3 to gain more GPIO pins.

Pin count audit:
- 3x voltage sense (one per panel) = 3 ADC pins
- 1x current sense = 1 ADC pin
- 2x I2C for light sensor = 2 pins
- 1x manual override button = 1 pin
- 2x motor drivers x 3 pins each (pulse, dir, enable) = 6 pins
- 1x MPPT PWM out = 1 pin
- 1x spray = 1 pin
- 1x brush = 1 pin
- USB CDC + bootstrap pins reserved

Total: 16+ functional GPIOs needed, plus reserves. ESP32-S3-WROOM-1 has 45 usable GPIOs which gives us plenty of headroom.

### Power Adjustments
- Added a 3.3V to 5V level shifter specifically for the motor drivers to ensure the logic signals are high enough.
- Added bypass capacitors for every IC and upgraded the main decoupling capacitor on the MCU to 2.2 uF.
- Added a 32 KHz crystal oscillator for the ESP32 timing.

### Sensor Adjustments
- Added low-pass filters to the op-amp outputs to clean up the voltage sense readings.
- Ensured 3.3V power is correctly routed to the op-amps.

### Current Sense Schematic Review
Reviewed the INA241A1xDDF current sense schematic. Noticed that the `solar_panel` node was left floating and unconnected to the main power path in the schematic capture. We will correctly route this directly to the high side of the shunt resistor.

Discussed the MPPT circuit further, analyzing the internal architecture of the current sense IC to better understand its differential measurement across the shunt.
