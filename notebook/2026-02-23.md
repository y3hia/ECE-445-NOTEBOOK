# 2026-02-23

## Objective
Finalize op-amp filtering for the voltage sensing circuit.

## Record of what was done
- Discussed the need for noise filtering on the OPA376 voltage sense amplifiers.
- Because the MPPT buck converter switches at high frequencies, the raw voltage divider signals will likely pick up significant switching noise.
- Researched standard RC low-pass filter configurations to place between the voltage divider network and the non-inverting input of the op-amp.

## Decision
Selected a 10 kOhm series resistor and a 10 pF bypass capacitor to ground. This RC combination will filter out high-frequency transients without causing excessive phase delay in the voltage readings required by the MPPT algorithm.

## Math
- Cutoff frequency: f_c = 1 / (2π × R × C) = 1 / (2π × 10kΩ × 10pF) ≈ 1.59 MHz
- Well above the 50 kHz buck switching frequency, so the algorithm sees the actual panel voltage with minimal lag.
- Below RF noise floors that could couple in from chassis or external sources.
