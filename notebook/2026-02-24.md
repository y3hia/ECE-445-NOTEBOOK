# 2026-02-24

## Objective
Meeting with Dr. Stillwell and final PCB parts specification.

## Record of what was done
Reviewed the current sense and gate drive strategy for the custom buck converter.

### Design Changes Implemented
- Decided to replace the current sense resistor with a much smaller value (approximately 10 mΩ) to minimize power dissipation at high currents.
- Swapped the current sense amplifier to a 50 V/V variant to match the new shunt value.
- Set the MOSFET gate resistance to 10 Ω (using a fixed 0603 resistor instead of a variable one) to control the switching speed.
- Selected a Vishay IHLP series inductor for the buck converter.

### Testing Strategy
Added specific pin headers (gH, gL, and GND) to the PCB so we can easily connect the MCU to the ECE 469 power board for initial testing before spinning our own buck converter.

### PCB Footprint and Routing Finalization
- Officially settled on the ESP32-S3-WROOM-1 microcontroller for the final board.
- Officially settled on the OPA376 for the precision op-amps.
- Modified the MCU layout to include pin headers for the external light sensors, spray pump controls, and brush motor controls.
- Added pin headers for limit switches and encoders to track the gantry position.
- Added the buck converter and MPPT circuitry to the board, alongside missing footprints for the linear voltage regulators.

## Power Math
- Shunt power dissipation at full current: P = I²R = 2.5² × 0.01 = 62.5 mW (acceptable)
- Sense voltage at full current: V = IR = 2.5 × 0.01 = 25 mV
- After 50 V/V gain: 1.25 V into the ADC, comfortably below the 3.3V max
