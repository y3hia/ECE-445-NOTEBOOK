# 2026-03-02

## Objective
Finalize current sense amplifier selection for PCB power sensing pathways.

## Record of what was done
- Reviewed initial recommendations from the design review (LT1999) against project requirements for the MPPT buck converter.

## Analysis
The LT1999 is solid, but given the high-frequency switching noise (100 kHz) we expect from the custom buck converter and the inductive spikes from the system, we need exceptional PWM rejection and a high common-mode voltage tolerance.

## Decision
Opted to swap the LT1999 for the **INA241B**. Selected the INA241B5 variant specifically for its 200 V/V gain to maximize ADC resolution on the ESP32. Verified that its -5V to +110V common-mode range will easily survive our 12V rails.

## Why INA241B over LT1999
- Higher PWM rejection (50 V/V vs. variable on LT1999)
- Designed specifically for switching power supply applications
- Common-mode range that comfortably handles back-EMF and inductive spikes
- 200 V/V gain option fits our small-shunt strategy
