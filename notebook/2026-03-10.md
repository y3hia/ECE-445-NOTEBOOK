# 2026-03-10

## Objective
Breadboard testing of the MPPT PWM logic and component sizing.

## Record of what was done
- Set up the ECE 469 power board to act as our buck converter.

## Test Parameters
- Input Voltage: 10 V
- PWM Signal: 0 to 5 V (level shifted), Frequency = 100 kHz, Duty Cycle (D) = 0.5

## Results & Adjustments
- Noticed some ringing on the MOSFET gates. Adjusted the gate resistor (Rgate) to approximately 40 Ω.
- This successfully minimized the gate ripple, though it caused a slight decrease in overall efficiency and output voltage.
- Measured output voltage (Vout) stabilized at approximately 4.9 V.

## Inductance Calculation
Calculated required inductance to ensure Continuous Conduction Mode (CCM) during standard operations:

L = (Vin - Vout) × D / (ΔIL × fsw)
L = (12 - 6) × 0.5 / (0.6 × 100,000)
L = 50 µH

## Decision
Will specify a 47 µH power inductor for the final PCB build (closest standard value).

## Open Issues
- 40 Ω gate resistor is high. May need to revisit when we move to the final board with proper gate driver instead of bench function generator.
