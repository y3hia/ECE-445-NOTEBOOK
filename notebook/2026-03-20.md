# 2026-03-20

## Objective
Troubleshoot INA241B current sense amplifier readings on the custom PCB.

## Record of what was done
- Tested the current sense test point with a multimeter; read 0V while under load.
- Discovered that applying physical pressure to the IC yielded a reading on the serial monitor, pointing to a mechanical connection failure (cold solder joint).
- Resoldered a new INA241B chip.
- After resoldering, the serial monitor showed readings, but only when a finger was placed near the chip.

## Analysis
The input pins (IN+ and IN-) were floating and acting as antennas due to the high gain (200 V/V), picking up 60Hz body noise.

## Solution
- Performed a continuity test from the physical shunt resistor to the IC legs.
- Verified the trace was electrically connected end-to-end.
- Ensured REF1 and REF2 pins were properly tied to ground for unidirectional sensing.

## Lessons Learned
- High-gain current sense ICs are extremely sensitive to floating inputs. Always verify continuity end-to-end before assuming the IC itself is broken.
- Body capacitance is a real diagnostic tool: if touching a pin changes the reading, the pin is likely floating.
