# 2026-03-24

## Objective
ESP32 power isolation and PCB layout strategy.

## Record of what was done
- Group meeting to finalize the routing strategy for the main control board before the final ordering deadline.

## Analysis
The DM542T stepper drivers and the proposed pump will draw heavy, noisy currents. If their return paths share the same ground plane area as the ESP32's ADC pins, ground bounce will ruin the MPPT voltage/current readings.

## Decision
Implemented a **Star Ground topology** in the PCB layout. The high-current 12V return path and the 3.3V logic return path will only meet at a single point right at the main power input terminal.

Added bulk decoupling capacitors (470 µF) to the 12V rail to act as a local energy reservoir, protecting the ESP32's onboard linear regulator from brownouts during initial motor/pump inrush.

## Why This Matters
- The ADC on the ESP32 is referenced to AGND. If AGND bounces because heavy stepper currents are flowing through it, the ADC sees a "moving zero" and reads garbage.
- Star ground keeps logic and power returns separate until they meet at a single low-impedance point, minimizing the loop area where ground bounce can corrupt readings.
