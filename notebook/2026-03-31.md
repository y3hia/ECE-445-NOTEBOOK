# 2026-03-31

## Objective
Indoor Solar Panel and Stepper Motor Testing.

## Record of what was done
Conducted baseline solar panel tests indoors (4024 lab at night) using flashlights and a halogen lamp setup.

## Panel 1 Results
- Flat on desk: 6.4 V
- Flat with halogen lamp: 11.6 V, with a short circuit current (Isc) of 0.5 A

## Panel 2 Results
- Flat on desk: 2.6 V
- Flat with halogen lamp: 11.2 V, with an Isc of 0.85 A

## Panel 3 Results
- Flat on desk with halogen lamp: 23.9 V open circuit (Voc), with an Isc of 0.25 A

## Observation
Panel 3 produces nearly twice the open-circuit voltage of the other two panels under the same lighting. This means the panel is likely a different model or has been wired differently internally. Will need to design a custom voltage divider with a different ratio for the Panel 3 sense line.

## Stepper Motor Test Procedure
- Tested the horizontal gantry motor utilizing a 24V DC supply bus (+VDC) and a function generator to supply the PWM control signal.
- Wired the motor phases specifically:
  - Red to Phase A+
  - Blue to Phase A-
  - Green to Phase B+
  - Black to Phase B-
- Grounded the PUL-, DIR+, DIR-, and ENA- pins to 0V.
- The ENA+ pin was intentionally left unconnected.
- Configured the DM542T onboard DIP switches 1 through 8 to set the current limits and microstepping resolution.
- Final DIP configuration: Off, On, Off, On, Off, On (7 and 8 unspecified).
- Sent a 0 to 5V square wave from the function generator at a 50% duty cycle and 4 kHz directly to the PUL+ pin.

## Result
Observational load testing confirmed 4 kHz yields the optimal linear velocity for the gantry to clean effectively without skipping steps.
