# 2026-04-02

## Objective
Outdoor Solar Panel Testing and Voltage Divider Calibration.

## Record of what was done
Moved the panels outdoors to measure actual operating voltages under sunlight.

## Measured Panel Voltages
- V1 to Ground = 10.32 V
- V2 to Ground = 18.33 V
- Did not test Panel 3 because a step-down resistor is still needed (the panel is rated for 24 Voc instead of 12 V).

## Voltage Divider Calibration
Measured the stepped-down sense voltages arriving at the MCU pins:
- Pin 1 to MCU: 0.912 V
- Pin 2 to MCU: 0.734 V

## Calculated Ratios
Computed the exact ratios to hardcode into the ESP32 logic so it can reconstruct the true panel voltages.

V1_sense / V1_panel = 0.912 / 10.32 = 0.08837
V2_sense / (V1_panel + V2_panel) = 0.734 / 18.33 = 0.04004

So the firmware needs to multiply by:
- Panel 1: ~11.31x to recover real voltage from V1 sense
- Cumulative tap (V1+V2): ~24.97x to recover from V2 sense

To get Panel 2 alone in firmware: subtract the recovered Panel 1 voltage from the recovered cumulative voltage:
**V_panel2 = (V2_sense × 24.97) - (V1_sense × 11.31)**

## Lessons
- The two dividers are sized very differently because they're protecting against different maximum voltages (one panel vs. cumulative two panels).
- Subtraction in the real voltage domain works cleanly as long as both dividers are calibrated independently.
