# 2026-04-05

## Objective
Resolve over-voltage issue on the MPPT Buck Converter output.

## Record of what was done
- Set the ESP32 to output a constant 50% duty cycle (D = 0.50).
- With a 12V input, expected output was 6V. Actual measured output was 6.6V.
- Checked the ESP32 PWM pin with an oscilloscope; verified a clean 49.7% duty cycle.

## Analysis
Because the buck converter was being tested open-circuit (without the load of the motors or pump), the average current requirement was near zero. This caused the inductor current to hit zero, forcing the converter into Discontinuous Conduction Mode (DCM) and slowly pumping up the output capacitor voltage.

## Solution
Attached a dummy power resistor across the output to draw continuous current. This forced the converter back into Continuous Conduction Mode (CCM), stabilizing the output at the expected 6V.

## Lessons Learned
- A buck converter without a minimum load will almost always overshoot in DCM.
- Either size the inductor for very low ripple (so DCM is harder to trigger), or always test under realistic load.
- For final integration this won't matter because the brush motor and pump will provide constant load.

## Notes
- The DCM-to-CCM boundary current can be calculated from inductor and switching frequency parameters.
- For our 47 µH inductor at 100 kHz switching, the boundary current is around 30 mA. Any load below that puts us in DCM territory.
