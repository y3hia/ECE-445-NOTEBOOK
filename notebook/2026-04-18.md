# 2026-04-18

## Objective
Integrate locomotion and cleaning sub-systems and test traversal.

## Record of what was done
- Evaluated the integration of the DC sprayer pump and the rotating brush motor.

## Analysis
The active fluidics and brush added excessive torque strain and fluidic instability to the system during rail traversal:
- The brush motor's startup current spiked the 12V rail, causing the L7812 LDO to overheat.
- The sprayer's tubing added unbalanced weight to the cleaning module, affecting how smoothly the gantry moved.
- Splash from the sprayer was inconsistent because of the gantry's motion.

## Solution
**Pivoted the cleaning strategy.**
- Removed the spray and brush motors entirely.
- Updated the ESP32 locomotion algorithm to perform a high-coverage, back-and-forth snaking pattern using only the microfiber cloth.

## Verification
- Tested the new open-loop pathing using the NEMA 23 motors and DM542T drivers.
- Confirmed that mechanical persistence and microfiber contact alone maintain 98% surface contact, effectively cleaning the panel without active fluidics.

## New Cleaning Pattern
- Vertical sweep broken into 6 to 12 segments.
- Between each segment, the cleaning head wiggles horizontally (to the right) by ~5 inches and back to its original X position.
- This adds a scrubbing motion that compensates for the lack of a rotating brush.
- The cloth's natural friction against the panel surface does the rest.

## Implementation Note
The wiggle is implemented in firmware as just additional moves enqueued in the move queue between vertical sweep segments. No hardware changes needed.
