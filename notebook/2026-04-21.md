## Power Failure Event & Troubleshooting
- When we tried to connect the solar panels and shone light on them, the 24 V barrel jack immediately started smoking.
- We disconnected power and troubleshot a major power failure.
- When using the 24V barrel jack, we lost the 5V and 3.3V rails completely.
- Also noticed the test point for the 3rd panel was not working.
- Discovered the 24V to 12V LDO (L7812) was shorted and continuously blowing up.
- We were running the gate driver off the 12V bus, and when we increased the panel voltage with the red halogen lamp, the LDO failed.

## Major Design Flaw Identified
The gate driver, brush motor, and water pump all require 12V, which greatly exceeds the 1.5A rating of the L7812 linear LDO.

Linear LDOs dissipate the voltage difference as heat:
- 24V in, 12V out, at 1A means 12V × 1A = 12W of waste heat.
- The L7812 thermal limit is reached well before 1.5A in any practical heatsinking situation.

## Discussion
- Replace the LDO with a proper 24V to 12V DC-DC buck converter chip in future revisions.
- Buck converters are 80%+ efficient instead of dissipating the difference, and they have proper thermal management built in.
- Common candidates: TI LMR62421, MP1584, TPS54331.

## Temporary Solution
- Replaced both the barrel jack and the L7812 components.
- After replacing them, we successfully operated the board with the 24 V barrel jack plugged in.
- Found all 4 DC bus voltages (24V, 12V, 5V, 3.3V) to be completely stable.
