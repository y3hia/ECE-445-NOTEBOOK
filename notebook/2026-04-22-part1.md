# 2026-04-22 — Part 1

## Objective
Evaluate solar panel string performance under load and test MPPT code.

## Record of what was done
Tested the solar panels wired in series. Found that one panel displayed a negative voltage, which brought down the total string voltage whenever a load was connected.

## Indoor Tests (no load, indoor lab)
- Vp1 ≈ 6 V
- Vp2 ≈ 2.5 V
- Vp3 ≈ 6.5 V
- Sum: roughly 15 V

## Under Load
- When adding a load resistor, the total string voltage drooped to about 2 V.
- With added light, the total string voltage rose to about 25 V.
- When we connected the external inductor (L) and resistor (R) for the buck converter, it drooped to roughly 7 V.

## Buck Converter Load Test (Running MPPT Code)
### Parameters
- R = 50 ohms
- L = 300 µH

### Results
- Under a halogen lamp, Vstring held at 6 V, and we measured Vout at 2.25 V.
- We removed one light source, and Vout dropped accordingly to 1.2 V.

## Analysis
- The negative voltage panel is concerning. Likely either a wiring polarity issue or a damaged cell.
- The MPPT is tracking, but with very low input power the duty cycle is being driven near its lower bound.
- This is a good sanity check that the algorithm responds to changing irradiance even at low power levels.
