# 2026-04-22 — Part 2

## Objective
Motor movement calculations and active brush module failure analysis.

## Motor Testing Calculations
Evaluated stepper performance at 6400 steps per revolution with a 4000 Hz 50% duty cycle signal.

- Step rate: 4000 pulses/sec
- Steps per revolution: 6400
- Revolutions per second: 4000 / 6400 = 0.625
- Linear travel: 7.5 inches per 3 seconds = **2.5 inches per second**

For the motors to cover one 13"x22" panel:
- Horizontal: 13 inches / 2.5 ips = 5.2 seconds
- Vertical: 22 inches / 2.5 ips = 8.8 seconds

This 2.5 inches/second figure is what gets hardcoded as `SPEED_IPS` in the firmware to convert distance commands into motor-on time.

## Brush Motor Failure Analysis
During testing with the brush motor on the cleaning module (which consists of the brush motor, chassis, and microfiber cloth), we found that it spun for approximately 3 to 4 seconds before stopping completely.

- The brush motor uses a 12V DC input to spin.
- We observed that our 24V to 12V LDO burned up after we tried using the brush motor.
- We believe this occurred because the brush motor is pulling too much current for the LDO to handle.
- The L7812 has a current rating of 1.5 A, which the DC motor exceeded.

## Confirmation
This is consistent with our 04-21 power failure analysis. The L7812 simply cannot supply continuous current to the brush motor under load. Confirmed plan to remove the brush motor entirely and rely on the wiggle motion in firmware.
