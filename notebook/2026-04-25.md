# 2026-04-25

## Objective
MPPT logic and PWM parameter testing.

## Record of what was done
Performed isolated tests utilizing a function generator to supply the PWM signal.

## Test 1
- Set a 25% duty cycle at 3.3 V and 50 KHz.
- Measured Vin from the panels at 8.2 V.
- Resulting Vout to the resistors: 1.68 V.

## MPPT Sensing Verification
- Measured Vin from panels at 15.3 V.
- Vmid (output) at 1.6 V.
- The controller settled in a duty cycle range of 0.07 to 0.09.

## Second Test
- Measured Vin from the panels at 18 V.
- Vout at 3 V.
- The duty cycle stabilized in the 0.185 to 0.2 range.

## Analysis
- The P&O algorithm is converging on different duty cycle ranges depending on input conditions, which is exactly what it should do.
- Higher input voltage requires a smaller duty cycle to produce a target output, and the algorithm finds that automatically.
- The "settling band" of 0.07 to 0.09 (or 0.185 to 0.2) is the expected oscillation around the maximum power point, not a bug.

## Implementation Note
The PWM hardware on the ESP32 LEDC module had to be configured to 50 kHz with 8-bit resolution, since 100 kHz at 10-bit was not achievable on this clock.

Math: at the LEDC clock of 80 MHz:
- 100 kHz × 1024 = 102.4 MHz needed → infeasible.
- 100 kHz × 256 = 25.6 MHz → feasible at 8-bit.
- Or 50 kHz × 1024 = 51.2 MHz → feasible at 10-bit.

Either choice works. Went with 50 kHz / 10-bit for finer duty resolution, and accepted the slightly lower switching frequency.
