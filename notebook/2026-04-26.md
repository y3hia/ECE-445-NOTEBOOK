## Sprayer Failure Analysis
- We found that the water sprayer has an inrush current of up to 0.9 A.
- This is too high for our rated switch current limits.
- Our 5V signal coming from the level shifter is rated for only 75 mA, far below 900 mA.
- Triggering the sprayer through the level shifter would damage the IC immediately.

## Conclusion
- Cannot get the water sprayer working safely for the final demo.
- I disassembled the sprayer entirely and physically removed the wires connecting it and the brush motor to the chassis.

## Future Fix
- Use a MOSFET switch driven by a logic-level GPIO to gate 5V or 12V to the sprayer.
- The MOSFET handles the current; the GPIO only sources gate charge (microamps).
- This decouples current draw from logic-level limits entirely.

## Final Cleaning Strategy
- Microfiber cloth contact only.
- Wiggle motion in firmware compensates for the loss of active fluidics and active brushing.
- Tested visually: panels return to expected voltage levels after a sweep, validating the approach.
